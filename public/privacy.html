Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 30, 2026

PocketCraft ("the app") is developed and maintained by an independent developer. This privacy policy explains what data is collected, how it is used, and what choices you have when you use PocketCraft.

1. Data We Collect

PocketCraft collects the following types of anonymous data to improve the app:

  • App usage events — such as when a server is started, stopped, or when features are used
  • Crash reports — including stack traces, device model, and Android version
  • App performance data — such as server startup time and network connection speed
  • A randomly generated anonymous installation ID used to manage your relay connection

We do not collect your name, email address, phone number, or any other personally identifiable information unless you choose to sign in with Google (see Section 4).

2. Data We Do Not Collect

We do not collect, store, inspect, or transmit any of the following:

  • Minecraft world data or server content
  • Player usernames or UUIDs
  • In-game chat messages or commands
  • Any content hosted on your Minecraft server

3. Relay Infrastructure

To allow players to connect to your server over the internet, PocketCraft routes network traffic through a relay server hosted on Amazon Web Services. This relay forwards raw network packets only and does not inspect, store, log, or process any game content or player data. Relay connections are identified only by your anonymous installation ID.

4. Google Sign-In and Google Drive

If you choose to sign in with Google to use the cloud backup feature, PocketCraft will access your Google account with the following limited permissions:

  • Your name and email address — displayed in the app so you know which account is connected
  • Google Drive file access — limited only to files created by PocketCraft. We create a folder called "PocketCraft Backups" in your Drive and store server backup zip files there. We do not access, read, or modify any other files in your Google Drive.

Signing in with Google is entirely optional. The core server hosting features work without signing in.

5. Third Party Services

PocketCraft uses the following third party services, each with their own privacy policies:

  • Google Firebase Analytics — anonymous usage analytics
  • Google Firebase Crashlytics — crash reporting
  • Google Firebase Performance — app performance monitoring
  • Google Sign-In & Google Drive — optional cloud backup feature
  • mc-heads.net — Minecraft player avatar images loaded from their public API
  • Amazon Web Services — relay server infrastructure

6. Data Retention

Anonymous analytics and crash data is retained by Firebase according to their standard retention policies (up to 14 months for analytics data). Google Drive backup files are stored in your personal Google Drive and are under your full control — you can delete them at any time from within the app or directly from Google Drive.

7. Your Choices

  • Analytics: Anonymous analytics collection is enabled by default to help us improve the app. We do not currently offer an opt-out within the app but plan to add this in a future update.
  • Google Sign-In: Signing in is optional. You can sign out at any time from Settings → Cloud Backup → Sign out.
  • Backups: You can delete any backup from the app or directly from your Google Drive at any time.

8. Children's Privacy

PocketCraft does not knowingly collect personal data from children under the age of 13. If you are under 13, please ask a parent or guardian before using the app. If we become aware that a child under 13 has provided personal data, we will take steps to delete it.

9. Security

We take reasonable measures to protect the data we collect. Anonymous analytics data is transmitted securely to Firebase over HTTPS. Relay traffic is forwarded as-is and is not stored. Google Drive backups are stored securely in your personal Google account.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. We will notify users of significant changes through the app. The date at the top of this page reflects when the policy was last updated. Continued use of the app after changes means you accept the updated policy.
